**Key observations:**
- FY26A NII growth of +4.9% is the weakest in 6 years, driven by NIM compression (3.80% FY24 → 3.29% FY26A = -51bps over 2 years)
- The NIM decline reflects: (a) absorption of HDFC Ltd's high-cost borrowing book post-merger, (b) 125bps RBI rate cut cycle transmitting faster on floating-rate loans (~70% of portfolio) than on deposits, (c) secular CASA ratio erosion from 38.2% (FY24) to 34.2% (FY26A)
- 4QFY26 reported NIM: 3.38% (company) — NOTE: Citi calculates this as 7bps QoQ DECLINE on a fully loaded basis vs 3.38% company-reported figure (different denominator: company uses total assets, Citi uses interest-earning assets only)
- Consensus expects NIM recovery from 3.29% (FY26A) to 3.45% (FY28E) driven by: borrowing rundown, time deposit repricing, loan mix normalisation

**DATA RECONCILIATION:** Company-reported NIM = 3.38% (based on total assets); Calculated NIM per Citi = ~3.31% (interest-earning assets basis, 7bps lower). Both figures are valid — they use different asset bases. This report uses the IEA-based figure for comparability. All subsequent NIM references are IEA-based. [Source: Citi Apr 19, 2026 note; Company Q4FY26 presentation]

**Key deposit franchise observations:**
- FY26A deposit growth of 14.4% was 200-300bps ahead of system — market share gain confirmed

- Retail deposits: consistently 80%+ of total deposits
- Time deposits growing fastest (+15.5% YoY FY26); saving accounts slower

- Mortgage cross-sell: liability penetration moved from 36% to 50% of mortgage portfolio over 2.5 years post-merger; 60-65% of mortgage borrowers now pay EMI from HDFC Bank account

**Critical observation:** ROE declined from 16.9% (FY24) to 14.0% (FY26) — the primary source of de-rating. This is driven by:
1. Equity base nearly doubled post-HDFC Ltd merger (+57% YoY in FY24)
2. ROA compression from NIM dilution
3. Earnings growing at 10-11% vs equity growing faster in base period

The recovery path requires ROA to expand from 1.80% toward the 1.90-2.0% range while equity growth moderates. Consensus models this materialising by FY29-30.

## 2.7 KEY FINANCIAL FLAGS FOR DOWNSTREAM STAGES

**For Delta (Bear Case):**
- FY26A loan growth 12% vs system 13.5% — 200bps miss on stated guidance
- Retail loan growth only 6.5% YoY — half the corporate growth rate; balance sheet skewing wholesale
- CASA ratio at 34.2% (FY26A) vs 48.2% (FY22A) — structural CoF headwind
- ROE consensus path (14.0%→14.6%) is below pre-merger 16-17% range and implies permanent franchise value destruction if not recovered
- Consensus FY26A credit cost 0.83% vs company 4Q guidance 0.45-0.50% — definition inconsistency flagged

**For Hotel (Valuation):**
- Tangible BV/Share: Rs365.68 (FY26A); Rs404.76 (FY27E); Rs453.06 (FY28E)
- Net NPA-adjusted BV/Share: Rs358.42 (FY26A); Rs393.50 (FY27E) [S&P Capital IQ]
- EPS progression: Rs48.51 (FY26A) → Rs54.28 (FY27E) → Rs63.15 (FY28E)
- Sustainable ROA: 1.80% (now) → 1.95% (FY30E consensus) → management target ~2.0%
