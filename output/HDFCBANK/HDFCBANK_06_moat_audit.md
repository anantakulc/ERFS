## 6.1 COMPETITIVE MOAT ASSESSMENT

### 6.1.1 Moat Type Classification

**Primary moat: SWITCHING COSTS + NETWORK EFFECTS + COST ADVANTAGE**

HDFC Bank's moat is multi-layered — no single source but a self-reinforcing system:

| Moat Component | Evidence | Durability |
|---------------|---------|-----------|
| Switching costs — banking relationships | Salary accounts, mortgage cross-sell, cash management mandates | STRONG — 30-year lock-in on corporate relationships |
| Network effects — payments | 26-28% of card spends; 35-36% of merchant acquiring | STRONG — self-reinforcing as more merchants = more card users |
| Scale advantages — cost of funding | 9,700 branches; 100mn customers; deposit market share gains | STRONG — granularity of deposits creates low-cost stable funding |
| Brand / trust premium | Government, RBI, SEBI openly endorsed the bank post-chairman resignation | STRONG — regulatory support is itself a competitive asset |
| Data advantage | 30+ year relationship data on 100mn customers; AI platform building | EMERGING — early stage |
| Distribution — MSME #1 | #1 in 15/28 states; top-2 in 25/28 states | STRONG |

### 6.1.2 Moat Strength vs Peers

| Bank | Primary Moat | Moat Rating | ROA (FY26 est.) | P/BV (FY26A) |
|------|-------------|-------------|-----------------|--------------|
| HDFC Bank | Switching costs + scale + network | WIDE | 1.80% | 2.04x |
| ICICI Bank | Distribution rebuild + technology | WIDE (improving) | ~2.3% | ~2.8-3.0x |
| Kotak Mahindra | CASA discipline + founder franchise | MODERATE | ~2.0% | ~3.0-3.5x |
| Axis Bank | Distribution + Citi integration | MODERATE | ~1.6-1.8% | ~2.0-2.2x |

**Critical observation:** HDFC Bank trades at a P/BV discount to ICICI Bank and Kotak — a reversal of the historical relationship. Pre-merger (FY22-FY23), HDFC Bank commanded 3.5-4.0x P/BV vs ICICI at 2.0-2.5x. The compression of this premium is the core investment thesis: is this permanent (moat degradation) or temporary (cyclical + governance)?

**Golf's verdict:** The moat is intact. The evidence:
1. Deposit market share gains of 200-300bps vs system in FY26 — even during the governance event, deposits did not flee
2. GNPA at 1.15% vs system 3%+ — 30 years of superior underwriting
3. The mortgage cross-sell achieving 50% liability penetration — stickiness is demonstrable
4. 26-28% card spend share — this is a network, not a franchise; it regenerates itself

The moat degradation thesis would require: (a) technology disruption by fintech/big-tech (e.g., Jio FS), (b) regulatory intervention on card/payment margins, (c) irreversible CASA erosion below 30%. None of these has occurred.

### 6.1.3 Moat Stress Tests

**Test 1: UPI / Digital Disruption**
HDFC Bank has 97% digital adoption for payments. The bank IS the digital channel for its customers. Jio Financial Services is a risk but HDFC Bank's 100mn customer base with 30-year relationships is a structural moat that takes a decade to erode.

**Test 2: Post-Merger HDFC Ltd Legacy**
The mortgage book cross-sell has been delivered: 50% liability penetration, EMI payment through HDFC Bank accounts by 60-65% of borrowers, CASA balances from ex-HDFC customers up from Rs50,000 cr to Rs86,000 cr over 2.5 years. The merger synergy is real.

**Test 3: Talent / Management Risk**
This is the most genuine moat risk. HDFC Bank's culture and underwriting discipline were built under Aditya Puri (founding CEO) and have continued under Jagdishan. If Jagdishan departs without a credible successor who maintains the culture, the moat becomes more brittle at the edges.

### 6.3.3 Credit Cost — FY26A Discrepancy

| Source | FY26A Credit Cost |
|--------|------------------|
| S&P Capital IQ (Visible Alpha) | 0.83% |
| Company guidance | 0.45-0.50% |
| 4QFY26 actual exit rate | 0.35% |
| FY25A actual | 0.45% |

**Reconciliation:** The S&P Capital IQ 0.83% credit cost in FY26A reflects the full-year income statement provision charge (Rs233,896M) divided by average net loans. The quarter-by-quarter distribution was highly uneven due to:
- 1QFY26: Rs90bn floating provision release (artificially low), then subsequent rebuild
- 2QFY26/3QFY26: normalisation
- 4QFY26: 0.35% exit rate

The company uses "credit cost (net of recoveries)" which consistently tracks 0.35-0.50%. The S&P Capital IQ 0.83% is a GAAP provision charge including standard asset provisioning and contingent provision movements — a broader definition. Both are correct under different accounting methodologies. FY27E consensus of 0.49% aligns with the "net credit cost" definition management uses. **This pipeline uses the company's net credit cost definition (0.35% 4QFY26, 0.45-0.50% FY26 guidance, 0.49% FY27E consensus) for forward modelling.**
