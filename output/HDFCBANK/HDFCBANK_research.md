## 2. COMPANY OVERVIEW & BUSINESS MODEL

HDFC Bank is India's largest private sector bank by assets, with a gross loan book of Rs29.4 trillion, deposit base of Rs31.1 trillion, and total assets of approximately Rs43.6 trillion as of FY26A. The bank serves over 100 million customers through 9,700+ branches and 21,000+ ATMs spanning urban, semi-urban, and rural India — a distribution franchise that no new entrant can replicate within a decade.

**The Merger That Transformed the Balance Sheet.** On 1 July 2023, HDFC Limited — India's largest housing finance company and HDFC Bank's founding promoter — merged into the bank, creating a combined entity nearly twice the pre-merger size. The merger transferred Rs7tn in mortgage receivables, Rs4.9tn in market borrowings (NCD, subordinated bonds, overseas debt), a 41% stake in HDFC Life Insurance, and 94.6% ownership of HDB Financial Services. The immediate structural consequence was a Loan-to-Deposit Ratio (LDR) spike to ~110% — a structural overhang now being systematically resolved through deposit-led expansion. LDR has declined from 110% (FY24) → 102% (FY25) → 95% (FY26), and management targets 85-88% by FY28.

**The Business Model Flywheel:**

## 3. LOAN BOOK COMPOSITION

HDFC Bank's Rs29.4 trillion gross loan book (FY26A, +12.1% YoY) reflects both organic growth and the HDFC Ltd mortgage inheritance. The post-merger mix carries a lower blended yield than the pre-merger HDFCBANK book, which is the primary NIM suppression mechanism. Management is deliberating re-balancing toward higher-yielding MSME and retail products.

| Segment | FY26A Share | FY26A (Rs tn) | YoY Growth |
|---------|------------|--------------|-----------|
| Retail (excl. mortgages) | ~30% | ~8.8 | +9% |
| Home Loans / Mortgages | ~23% | ~6.8 | +5% |
| MSME / Business Banking | ~12% | ~3.5 | +18% |
| Corporate / Wholesale | ~27% | ~7.9 | +16% |
| Agriculture & Agri-allied | ~8% | ~2.4 | +14% |
| **Total Gross Loans** | **100%** | **~29.4** | **+12.1%** |

**Segment analysis:**

- **Retail (excl. mortgages):** Cards, personal loans, auto, and consumer durables. The bank has deliberately slowed unsecured retail origination given RBI macro-prudential guidance and sector-wide delinquency uptick. Yields: 12-18%. NIM-accretive but volume-constrained.

- **Mortgages:** The merged HDFC Ltd book. Average yield ~8.0-8.5% — significantly below the blended retail product yield. This segment dilutes consolidated NIM by approximately 15-20bps. Modest growth (+5%) as the bank focuses on margin over volume in this segment.

- **MSME / Business Banking:** Highest-growth segment at +18% YoY. Yields 12-14%. Positive NIM mix-shift driver. Management has committed to 20%+ MSME growth over FY27-29, which will gradually offset mortgage mix drag.

- **Corporate / Wholesale:** Well-rated large corporate borrowers; yield 8.5-9.5%. LDR normalization has partly been achieved by growing wholesale loans faster (capital-efficient but NIM-dilutive). Corporate growth moderated as management is selective on pricing.

- **Agriculture:** Priority sector obligation; yield ~9-10%. Broadly stable mix.

**Loan Growth Guidance Miss — Elevated Concern:** Management guided 16-18% loan growth for FY26; delivered 12.1%. This is the second consecutive year of guidance miss (FY25: guided 15%, delivered ~10%). Delta (Stage 3) rates this a HIGH conviction bear pillar. The miss reflects a deliberate deposit-first strategy (prioritising LDR improvement) — but the market requires confirmation that this is strategic restraint, not competitive market share loss to ICICI and Kotak.

**Loan-to-Deposit Ratio:** Rs29.4tn / Rs31.1tn = **94.5% ≈ 95%**

**The LDR Normalization Thesis:** The single most important medium-term value unlock in HDFC Bank's re-rating story. From a peak of ~110% (FY24), the LDR has declined 15 percentage points over two years — pacing ahead of management's stated timeline. The target of 85-88% by FY28 implies an additional Rs2.5-3.5tn of deposit headroom absorption. As LDR falls, the bank:
1. Can grow loans at a higher rate without raising expensive bulk deposits
2. Will retire high-cost merger-era borrowings as they mature, improving funding mix
3. Will see NIM recovery as the borrowing book (at avg. cost ~7.0-7.5%) is replaced by lower-cost retail deposits

**CASA erosion — a watch item:** CASA has declined from 38.2% (FY24) → 35.8% (FY25) → 34.2% (FY26). The decline reflects two dynamics: (i) aggressive retail term deposit mobilisation (bank raised TD rates to attract deposits for LDR normalization); and (ii) post-merger structural mix change (HDFC Ltd had no CASA). CASA convergence toward ICICI (42%) and Axis (42%) represents a funding cost headwind. Stabilisation is expected at 34-35% as TD repricing pressure eases with the rate cycle.

**Borrowings legacy:** Rs4.9tn of market borrowings inherited from HDFC Ltd (NCD + subordinated bonds + overseas borrowings via the Dubai IFSC branch). Average cost ~7.0-7.5%. As these instruments mature over FY27-FY30, replacement with retail deposits will mechanically reduce cost of funds and restore NIM. Stage 6 Golf flagged the Dubai branch borrowing structure as an open disclosure item — not a risk per se, but warrants monitoring.

## 9. COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE

### 9A. Market Position

HDFC Bank operates in India's ~Rs210 trillion banking system, where private sector banks collectively hold ~38% of total credit. The bank commands approximately 14% of total private bank credit — the single largest share, ahead of ICICI (12%) and SBI (23% of total system but public sector).

**Addressable Market (India banking TAM):**
- Total system credit FY26: ~Rs215tn; system credit CAGR FY24-29E: ~13.5-14%
- Private bank credit: ~Rs82tn; growing faster than PSU banks at ~15-16% CAGR
- India nominal GDP CAGR FY24-30E: ~9-9.5%; credit/GDP penetration expanding from ~57% toward 65%
- Underpenetrated segments: MSME credit gap ~Rs27tn; agricultural credit gap ~Rs15tn

**Key competitive dynamics:**
- ICICI Bank gaining market share in retail unsecured (personal loans, credit cards) and picking up disenchanted HDFC Bank employees and relationship bankers
- Kotak Bank pivoting to mass market after regulatory restrictions on digital onboarding lifted
- Axis Bank executing on "GPS" strategy (Growth + Profitability + Sustainability) with NIM above sector
- IndusInd Bank structurally impaired (MFI exposure, governance concerns, NIM pressure) — creating a market share vacuum in urban SME

### 9B. Moat Assessment (Golf Stage 6: 4.2 / 5 — Wide Moat, Temporarily Impaired)

## 11. VALUATION

### 11A. Gordon Growth Model (GGM) Justified P/BV

The primary valuation methodology for Indian private sector banks.

**Formula:** Justified P/BV = (ROE − g) / (ke − g)

| Input | Value | Source |
|-------|-------|--------|
| ROE (sustainable, FY28E) | 14.8% | Stage 7 Hotel; consensus range 14-16% |

## 12. BULL CASE — 10 CATALYSTS

**Bull Case Target Price: Rs1,050 | Probability: 25%**

**CAT-1: CEO Renewal Confirmation (Oct 2026, Probability 65%, Price Impact +10-15%)**
The single largest near-term binary event. RBI approval of Sashidhar Jagdishan for a second term would immediately remove the governance overhang that has suppressed HDFC Bank's P/BV multiple. Jagdishan has guided the post-merger integration with capital discipline and NIM management, and his institutional knowledge of the HDFC Ltd balance sheet is irreplaceable at this juncture. A renewal announcement — expected by August-October 2026 — would likely trigger a rally to Rs830-860 on relief alone, independent of fundamentals. The bull case subsequently builds on an uninterrupted management continuity through FY29.

**CAT-2: NIM Inflection — 1QFY27 Results (July 2026)**
Three quarters of NIM stabilisation (3.29-3.31%) have built the floor narrative. The trigger for re-rating is a QoQ NIM expansion to ≥3.35% in 1QFY27, driven by: (i) HDFC Ltd borrowing maturities rolling off at lower replacement rates; (ii) MSME loan mix increasing; and (iii) savings deposit cost declining as TD repricing pressure eases. A 1QFY27 NIM print of 3.35-3.40% would validate the NIM recovery thesis and likely catalyse consensus estimate upgrades across the sector.

**CAT-3: Rate Cycle Easing (RBI, FY27)**
The RBI's rate-cutting cycle (repo at 5.75%, paused) has been constrained by India-Pakistan geopolitical tensions and domestic food inflation. Resumption of cuts — expected in Q3FY27 if inflation moderates — will: (i) increase bond portfolio MTM gains (~Rs10-15Bn per 25bps cut); (ii) allow lending rate reductions that drive loan demand; and (iii) improve HDFC Bank's MCLR-linked portfolio repricing dynamics. HDFC Bank is a structural beneficiary of a steepening yield curve given its fixed-rate asset mix.

**CAT-4: HDB Financial Services IPO (FY27-28)**
HDB Financial has filed its Draft Red Herring Prospectus (DRHP) and is awaiting SEBI approval. The IPO will: (i) crystallise the subsidiary's valuation on listed markets, enabling SOTP investors to price it directly; (ii) generate a one-time gain of Rs25-35Bn on the bank's books; and (iii) provide HDB with growth capital to scale its MSME / consumer lending book. Stage 7 values HDB at Rs49/HDFCBANK share. A successful IPO at a premium could increase this attribution to Rs70-80/share — adding Rs20-30 to the SOTP TP.

**CAT-5: LDR Normalization to ≤88% Ahead of Schedule**
Current LDR trajectory (110% → 95% in 24 months) paces well ahead of the FY28 management target. If the LDR reaches 88-90% by end-FY27 (18 months early), it would: (i) reduce the need for bulk/expensive deposit mobilisation; (ii) allow NIM to expand faster than consensus models; and (iii) unlock loan growth re-acceleration to 15-17% without creating funding stress. Consensus models are built on a 3-year normalisation path — a faster resolution would be a meaningful positive surprise.

**CAT-6: MSME Loan Acceleration — 20%+ Growth**
HDFC Bank's MSME / business banking segment is growing at 18% (FY26) and management has targeted 20%+ over FY27-29. MSME loans carry yields of 12-14% — approximately 400bps above the mortgage blended yield — and the segment represents the highest-marginal-NIM opportunity in the loan book. The bank has deployed 2,000+ dedicated MSME relationship managers and rolled out GST-linked digital underwriting. If MSME reaches 15% of the loan book (from 12% today) by FY29, the NIM benefit is +15-20bps — a structural, durable improvement.

**CAT-7: India-Pakistan Tensions De-escalation**
The active geopolitical tension between India and Pakistan (as of June 2026) has created risk-off pressure on India financials, with FPI outflows from the banking sector estimated at $1.5-2Bn in May-June 2026. A ceasefire and normalisation would reverse these flows, with large-cap Indian banks — HDFC Bank foremost — as the primary beneficiary of FPI inflows. Historical precedent (2019 Balakot episode): Indian banking stocks recovered 15-20% in 6-8 weeks post-de-escalation.

**CAT-8: Credit Card Market Share Stabilisation**
HDFC Bank's credit card market share (by outstanding) declined from 27% (FY22) to ~24% (FY26) as ICICI and Axis gained aggressively. Management has invested in co-branded partnerships (Swiggy, IRCTC, Marriott) and new card variants. Stabilisation of market share at 23-24% — or better, a recovery to 25% — would be a positive signal on retail franchise resilience and support fee income growth above consensus.

**CAT-9: Wholesale Deposit Repricing Windfall**
A meaningful portion of the Rs4.9tn borrowing legacy is indexed to floating rates tied to market yields. If RBI cuts repo by 75-100bps over FY27 (consensus: 50-100bps), the borrowing cost reduction on floating-rate instruments would be direct NIM uplift of +3-7bps — a small but meaningful mechanical improvement requiring no management action.

**CAT-10: HDFC Life / HDFC AMC Re-Rating**
HDFC Life trades at ~3x P/Embedded Value and HDFC AMC at ~35x P/E. Any sector re-rating in insurance (driven by rising ULIP demand post-equity bull market) or AMC (driven by SIP inflows sustaining >Rs200Bn/month) would flow through to HDFC Bank's SOTP valuation. A 20% re-rating in subsidiaries adds ~Rs23/share to HDFCBANK's SOTP TP.

## 13. BEAR CASE — 10 THESIS-BREAKERS

**Bear Case Target Price: Rs680 | Probability: 25%**

**BEAR-1: CEO Non-Renewal or Abrupt Departure (Oct 2026)**
If the RBI declines to renew Jagdishan's term — citing regulatory concerns or succession planning — the bank faces a leadership vacuum during the most operationally complex period of post-merger integration. An interim CEO with limited merger institutional knowledge could slow LDR normalization decisions, MSME growth plans, and HDB IPO execution. Delta estimates a 35% probability of non-renewal. Deep bear TP in this scenario: **Rs544** (-27% from current).

**BEAR-2: Loan Growth Structural Miss — Third Year (FY27)**
If HDFC Bank delivers sub-13% loan growth for a third consecutive year, it moves the debate from "strategic restraint" to "franchise deterioration." Citi (Stage 3 primary source) estimates ICICI and Kotak are capturing corporate banking clients and cross-selling the banking relationship. A third guidance miss would force consensus to lower FY28-29 earnings by 10-15% and de-rate the stock further toward 1.6-1.7x P/BV (Axis Bank territory).

**BEAR-3: NIM Fails to Recover Above 3.35% by FY27E**
The consensus recovery thesis requires NIM to reach 3.40-3.45% by FY28. If merger-era borrowing costs prove stickier than modelled (due to bank-specific credit spread widening in the bond market) and CASA continues to erode below 33%, NIM could stagnate at 3.25-3.30% through FY28 — compressing earnings by Rs8,000-10,000Cr versus bull consensus and limiting ROE expansion to 14-14.5%.

**BEAR-4: Retail Unsecured Credit Cost Normalisation**
The RBI's macro-prudential measures on unsecured credit (risk weight increase to 125% for personal loans and credit cards in November 2023) are showing early signs of stress normalisation in the sector. If GNPA in the cards + personal loan portfolio rises from current ~2.5-3.0% to 4-5% (comparable to 2019 stress levels), credit costs could rise to 0.50-0.55% of loans — adding Rs12,000-15,000Cr to annual provisions and compressing PAT by 15-20%.

**BEAR-5: CASA Structural Decline Below 32%**
CASA has fallen 4 percentage points in 24 months. If the rate cycle does not ease materially (sticky repo at 5.75%+), competitive pressures from small finance banks offering 8-9% savings rates could continue to drain HDFC Bank's retail savings book. CASA below 32% would mechanically raise cost of funds by 20-25bps and partly offset the NIM benefit of LDR normalisation.

**BEAR-6: HDB Financial IPO Fails or Is Delayed**
SEBI could raise objections to HDB Financial's DRHP given its exposure to high-risk MFI (microfinance) and consumer finance segments that are showing stress. A delayed IPO (FY29 or later) would: (i) defer Rs25-35Bn gain recognition; (ii) prevent subsidiary valuation crystallisation; and (iii) force HDB to rely on parent funding for growth (constraining HDFCBANK's own capital deployment).

**BEAR-7: Regulatory Risk — RBI Intervention**
HDFC Bank has historically had clean regulatory interactions. However, the Dubai IFSC branch (offshore borrowing vehicle inherited from HDFC Ltd) represents an open item that Golf (Stage 6) flagged. If RBI requires accelerated deleveraging of offshore borrowings or imposes restrictions on the Dubai branch, the bank's funding flexibility would be constrained and overseas cost of funds could rise.

**BEAR-8: India Macro Deterioration — Geopolitical Escalation**
The India-Pakistan geopolitical tensions (active as of June 2026) represent a tail risk. Further escalation — military conflict, trade disruption, capital flight — would: (i) trigger a broad banking sector sell-off; (ii) raise sovereign risk premia, increasing ke by 100-150bps; and (iii) suppress credit demand from corporates and SMEs. In a stress scenario, HDFCBANK P/BV could compress to 1.4-1.5x — implying Rs570-610/share.

**BEAR-9: ROE Remains Structurally Capped at 14-14.5%**
If LDR normalises but NIM recovery is offset by deteriorating loan mix (mortgage growth outpacing MSME) and rising credit costs, the bank's medium-term ROE ceiling may be 14-14.5% rather than the 16-17% pre-merger level. At 14% ROE, GGM justified P/BV (ke 11.5%, g 6.5%) = 1.50x × Rs453 = **Rs680** — which is the bear case TP and also the lower end of our valuation range.

**BEAR-10: Management Delivery Credibility Irreparably Damaged**
The two-year loan growth guidance miss pattern is building a Macquarie-style "Shifting Goalposts" narrative. If FY27 loan growth again misses guidance, the market may permanently discount management forward guidance, leading to a sustained valuation multiple compression. This is a slow-burning bear, not a binary event, but could cause the stock to underperform peers by 15-20% over 18-24 months.
