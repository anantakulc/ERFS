## 5.1 INDIA MACRO ENVIRONMENT — BASELINE (JUNE 2026)

### 5.1.1 GDP and Growth

India's macro backdrop as of June 2026 is constructive but subject to elevated geopolitical uncertainty:

- **Nominal GDP growth:** ~9-9.5% (management stated as baseline assumption for FY26 system credit calibration)
- **Real GDP growth:** ~6.5-7.0% (implied, consistent with long-run trend)
- **System credit growth (RBI, March 2026):** ~13.5-14% YoY — running above nominal GDP, reflecting financial deepening
- **India-Pakistan geopolitical tensions (March-April 2026):** Border escalation noted in Q4FY26 earnings call; management flagged "a couple of months" of potential corporate demand headwind; as of June 2026, situation is partially de-escalated but remains a risk variable
- **India's fiscal position:** Central government fiscal deficit ~4.5-5.0% of GDP (FY26 estimate); fiscal policy broadly supportive with infrastructure capex continuing

**HDFC Bank macro sensitivity:** The bank's loan portfolio is predominantly domestic India (minimal international exposure other than Dubai branch). Credit demand tracks nominal GDP with a multiplier. A 100bps decline in nominal GDP growth would be expected to reduce system credit growth by ~150bps and HDFC Bank loan growth by ~150-200bps.

### 5.1.2 Interest Rate Environment

- **RBI Repo Rate (June 2026):** 5.75% [implied from 125bps of cuts from 6.5% since Feb 2025; pre-loaded data confirms "125bps repo rate cuts since Feb'25"]
- **Rate cycle status:** PAUSED — India-Pakistan tensions drove oil price volatility and hardened government bond yields
- **10-year G-Sec:** Rising from ~6.5% to ~6.8-7.0% (implied from CFO Q4FY26 comment: "looking at the securities market, it seems to be going up")
- **Next MPC meeting:** Expected August 2026 — market pricing ~40% probability of additional 25bps cut conditional on conflict de-escalation
- **Terminal rate:** Most models suggest 50-75bps more of cuts over FY27 cycle, taking repo to 5.25-5.50%

**HDFC Bank rate sensitivity:**
- ~70% of loan book is floating rate (management disclosure) → rapid asset yield repricing on any additional cuts
- Deposit repricing: ~40-50bps of the 125bps cuts have transmitted so far (CFO Q4FY26 call); residual repricing of time deposits over 5-6 quarters will compress CoF further
- **Net NIM impact:** Each additional 25bps cut: approximately -5bps NIM in the first 2 quarters (asset repricing faster), then +3-5bps net as deposits reprice. The lag creates short-term NIM volatility.

## 5.2 REGULATORY ENVIRONMENT — INDIA BANKING (RBI)

### 5.2.1 LDR / LCR Regime

- **LDR is NOT a regulatory constraint:** RBI Governor explicitly stated this; RBI confirmed comfortable with HDFC Bank's funding profile (Macquarie, April 6, 2026)
- **LCR framework:** RBI proposed revisions to LCR computation in 2024 (higher run-off rates for digital deposits). If implemented, HDFC Bank's reported LCR of 114% could compress by 5-10 percentage points — still above the 100% minimum but reduces the buffer
- **RBI's forward guidance on deposit quality:** Regulators have been encouraging banks to focus on granular, retail deposits — aligned with HDFC Bank's stated strategy (47% of FY26 net time deposit accretion came from <Rs3 crore accounts)

### 5.2.2 Capital Adequacy — HDFC Bank Is Overcapitalised

- **CET1:** 17.30% (FY26A) vs minimum regulatory CET1 of ~8% (India Basel III); buffer of ~930bps
- **Total CAR:** 19.70% vs minimum 11.5-12%
- No capital-related regulatory risk; if anything, regulators could pressure dividend distribution
- **Risk:** Any new risk-weight revision (RBI previously increased risk weights on consumer credit in 2023) that materially inflates RWAs

### 5.2.3 NBFC Regulatory Risk (via HDB Financial)

- RBI's tightening of NBFC norms (higher NPA classification, risk weights, leverage limits) has created headwinds for the sector
- HDB Financial Services (HDFC Bank 74.12% stake) operates as an NBFC with Gross Stage 3 at 2.44% — above the parent bank
- RBI's requirement for large NBFCs to list (above a threshold size): HDB is subject to this requirement and is preparing for IPO
- Risk: If RBI further tightens NBFC capital norms, HDB may require capital infusion from HDFC Bank — this would not be material given the parent's capital strength but would create an earnings drag

### 5.2.4 Banking Sector Concentration / Competition Regulation

- RBI has historically been supportive of HDFC Bank's expansion
- Foreign shareholding cap: current limit appears accommodative (foreign ownership level referenced in CLSA note at 3QFY26: approximately 30-35%)
- PSU bank competition: SBI and other PSU banks have become more competitive in rural and semi-urban segments through Jan Dhan accounts and government scheme lending — but this primarily affects the Rs0-500K customer segment, not HDFC Bank's core

## 5.3 GEOPOLITICAL RISKS

### 5.3.1 India-Pakistan Tensions (Primary Active Risk — June 2026)

- **Background:** Escalation began Q1 2026 (referenced in Q4FY26 earnings call, HDFC Bank IR presentation safe harbour statement)
- **Economic impact:** Potential disruption to cross-border trade, investment confidence, oil price impact via Gulf route disruption
- **HDFC Bank specific:**
  - Trade finance volumes could temporarily soften (CFO flagged lower FX volumes and spreads in 4QFY26)
  - Corporate capex may slow: "we will have to temper it given the fallout of what we see in the geopolitical area, which hopefully should not be more than a couple of months" (DMD Kaizad, Q4FY26 call)
  - Defence sector and infrastructure spending may accelerate → offset
  - Management assessment: limited to "a couple of months" impact as of April 18, 2026 → increasingly dated as of June 2026; conflict continuation would extend the headwind

**Delta bear trigger activated:** If India-Pakistan conflict escalates materially (e.g., broader military engagement), impact on SME credit demand, corporate confidence, and system liquidity could push credit growth below 12% and credit costs above 0.55%.

### 5.3.2 Gulf Conflict / Oil Price Risk (Secondary)

- HDFC Bank Q4FY26 presentation explicitly references the "war in Iran, involving the United States, Israel and some neighbouring countries"
- India imports ~85% of its oil needs — a sustained oil price spike >$100/barrel would:
  - Widen India's current account deficit
  - Pressure the INR (Rs/USD at ~83-85 currently)
  - Complicate RBI's ability to cut rates (imported inflation)
  - Hit NRI deposit flows (Gulf NRI deposits are a meaningful part of HDFC Bank's NRI portfolio)
- Macquarie noted "post-Gulf conflict cost pressures on wholesale deposits" in April 2026
- HDFC Bank's LDR declined in Q4 partly because of strong EOP deposits — some of this may be Gulf-war related liquidity seeking safety in Indian banks

### 5.3.3 US Trade Tariffs / Global Growth

- India-US trade relationship: India is a beneficiary of the "China+1" manufacturing thesis
- US tariff regime (2025-2026): India has been partially insulated due to trade negotiations; pharmaceutical and IT services exports from India remain broadly intact
- HDFC Bank exposure: Limited direct exposure; indirect via corporate loan book to export-oriented sectors

## 5.4 INDIA BANKING SYSTEMIC RISKS

### 5.4.1 Credit Cycle Risk

India's credit cycle has been broadly benign through FY24-FY26:
- System GNPA ratio declined from ~9% (FY18-19) to ~3% (FY26 estimate)
- PSU bank recapitalisation + IBC resolution process has cleaned up the legacy NPA cycle
- New risks emerging: microfinance (MFI) sector stress, unsecured personal loans, rural credit

**HDFC Bank's position:** Relatively insulated due to:
- Predominantly secured retail book (mortgages, auto loans)
- Strong underwriting standards — 30-year track record through 3 business cycles (management boast)
- 1.15% GNPA vs system 3%+ — already a significant divergence

### 5.4.2 Digital Payments Disruption

- UPI volume growing >30% annually; Jio Financial Services (Reliance) is building a competing banking and lending platform
- PhonePe, Google Pay, and Paytm continue to drive zero-MDR payment volumes
- HDFC Bank's response: Zapp (full-stack UPI wallet), OTP-less authentication, PayZapp
- Risk: If HDFC Bank loses transaction banking primacy due to big-tech competition, CASA balances and fee income would erode further

### 5.4.3 Climate / Environmental Risk

- Long-term risk for agricultural lending portfolio (ex-agri GNPA already 24bps better than headline)
- HDFC Bank has committed to carbon neutrality target
- ESG score: 80/100 (Sustainalytics); MSCI EM Extended ESG Focus Index
- Climate risk is a FY30+ consideration; not material to the 12-18 month investment horizon
