## What each company does (plain English)

Think of these 7 companies as different layers of the global technology economy:

| # | Ticker | Simple Description |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **NVDA** | Designs the AI "brains" (GPUs) — every AI model runs on these |
| 2 | **AVGO** | Builds custom AI chips for Google/Meta/Apple + networking silicon; also owns VMware software |
| 3 | **TSM** | The world's only factory that can build advanced chips — makes chips for NVDA, Apple, AMD |
| 4 | **ASML** | Makes the only machines capable of printing advanced chips — a true monopoly |
| 5 | **AMAT** | Makes other chip factory equipment — sells to every semiconductor factory |
| 6 | **GEV** | Makes gas turbines, wind turbines & power grid equipment — supplies the electricity AI data centers need |
| 7 | **FN** | Assembles optical/fiber parts that connect AI servers together inside data centers |

**GEV is the odd one out** — it's an industrial/energy company, not a semiconductor name.
It plays the AI theme from the "power" angle: every AI data center needs massive amounts
of electricity, and GEV makes the turbines and grid gear that produce and deliver it.

## The Ranking — Best to Worst Single Investment

### 🥇 #1 NVDA — Best overall risk/reward right now

**Why it wins:** NVDA is simultaneously the fastest-growing AND the cheapest company in
this group on forward earnings. A 16.1× forward P/E for +85% revenue growth is a
near-impossible combination for a $5 trillion company. The PEG of 0.63 means you're
paying 63 cents for every dollar of earnings growth — dirt cheap.

- $46B FCF per year — best absolute cash machine in the group
- 95% of 59 analysts say Buy; mean target $299 (+46%)
- At $205, RSI 42, sitting on 50-day moving average — technically clean entry
- Down 13% from high, not overbought

**Risk:** Most volatile stock of the 7 (beta 2.20). Export control risk on China. If AI
spending slows, this is the stock that gets hit hardest. Bear case: $100.

**Best entry:** $185–205 (buyable now). Add more at $165–175 if it dips.

---

### 🥈 #2 AVGO — Biggest surprise, best upside target

**Why it's #2:** Broadcom is the hidden gem of this list. It has:
- The **highest gross margin** of all 7 (76% — even higher than NVIDIA's 74%)
- Second-cheapest forward P/E (19.7×) for the second-fastest AI revenue growth (+48%)
- PEG of 0.69 — almost as cheap as NVDA relative to its growth
- Down **23% from its 52-week high** ($495 → $382) — the biggest pullback of the group
- Analyst mean target of **$522** — highest absolute upside (+37%) of all 7 stocks

**What AVGO does that's different from NVDA:** While NVIDIA makes general-purpose GPUs,
Broadcom makes *custom* AI chips (called XPUs or ASICs) specifically designed for
individual customers. Google's TPU (used to train Gemini), Meta's MTIA, and Apple's
neural engine components are all built with Broadcom's help. AVGO also owns VMware
(enterprise software), which adds stable, recurring revenue that protects margins even
if the AI chip cycle slows.

**The one big risk:** AVGO carries **$45B net debt** from buying VMware. With $27B/year
in FCF it's manageable (debt paid off in ~1.7 years of FCF), but it limits flexibility.

**Best entry:** $370–400 (near current 40-day support). Great entry zone right now.

---

### 🥉 #3 TSM — Best quality business, slightly wrong price

**Why it's #3:** Best moat of any company here — the irreplaceable foundry. AVGO, NVDA,
Apple, AMD all depend on TSMC. But at $424 it's sitting about 8% above our intrinsic
value ($390) after a +99% run in the past 12 months. Too good to skip, too expensive to
chase right now.

**Wait for:** $370–390 (below intrinsic value). A pullback to the 200-day MA ($331)
would be a gift.

---

### #4 GEV — Completely different but worth owning for diversification

**Why it makes the list:** GE Vernova is the only non-semiconductor stock here, and that's
exactly why it's interesting. It plays AI from the **electricity angle**: every ChatGPT
response, every image generated, every AI model trained consumes huge amounts of power.
GEV makes gas turbines (for always-on power), wind turbines (for clean energy), and grid
equipment (to transmit that power). AI data centers are one of its fastest-growing customer
segments.

**The numbers:**
- Down 20% from its high ($1,182 → $941), RSI 36 — near oversold, getting attractive
- Analyst mean target $1,212 (+29% upside)
- Backlog is enormous — GEV has multi-year order books that provide revenue visibility
- ROE of 76% — exceptional for an industrial company
- Low operating margin (5.5%) today, but rapidly expanding as orders convert to revenue

**The catch:** Thin margins (20% gross, 5.5% operating), Fwd PE of 38.4× is expensive
for 16% revenue growth. Not a screaming buy — but a useful **portfolio diversifier** that
hedges you away from pure semiconductor risk.

**Best entry:** $860–900 (near 40-day support, approaching oversold on RSI).

---

### #5 ASML — World's best monopoly, wrong price

ASML makes the only machines (EUV lithography) that can print advanced chips. No
competitor exists or can exist for at least a decade. But at $1,864, the stock is near
its all-time high with analyst targets *below* current price ($1,702, −9%). Expensive at
38.8× forward P/E for only 13% growth. Wait for a meaningful pullback.

**Best entry:** $1,550–1,650 (50-day support zone).

---

### #6 FN — Small bet, low quality

Fabrinet assembles fiber-optic transceivers for AI networking. Pure AI exposure but it's
just an assembler — 12% gross margins, negative FCF, no moat. The smallest, weakest
business of the 7. Only for high-risk tolerance investors who want AI networking leverage.

**Best entry:** $540–580.

---

### #7 AMAT — Solid company, terrible timing

Applied Materials is a great semiconductor equipment company — but right now it's at its
52-week high (RSI 77, dangerously overbought) with analyst targets **below** current
price (−9%). It will likely pull back. Not a buy today.

**Best entry:** $460–490 (wait for the pullback to the 50-day MA).

## If you can only pick ONE from all 7: **NVDA**

Same answer as before, reinforced. Adding AVGO and GEV doesn't change the top pick —
NVDA remains the standout: cheapest relative to growth, largest FCF, strongest AI moat
via CUDA software lock-in, near technical support, and with the highest earnings beat
streak.

**But AVGO is the new discovery here.** If you're building a 2-stock portfolio from this
list, pair **NVDA + AVGO**: complementary AI exposure (GPU computing vs custom ASICs),
both cheap on forward earnings, both at attractive pullback entries right now.
