## Bull Case in Three Points

**1. AI ASIC Franchise — Durable, Multi-Year Design Wins**
Three hyperscalers (Alphabet TPU, Meta MTIA, Apple server inference) are co-designing next-generation AI accelerators with Broadcom under multi-year agreements with high switching costs. Q2 FY26 AI revenue run-rate = ~$4.4B/quarter (~$17B+ annualized). FY26E total revenue consensus = $105.97B (+66% YoY). FY27E = $169.72B. These are not spot-market wins — they are 3–5 year co-design engagements.

**2. VMware: The World's Most Profitable Software Franchise**
Infrastructure Software segment generated ~$33.8B revenue at ~70–75% EBITDA margin in FY25. Broadcom converted VMware to subscription/SaaS with $13.0B in deferred revenue providing visibility. At 22x EBITDA, the software segment alone is worth $530–600B in EV — more than the current total enterprise value of $1.876T minus the semiconductor segment.

**3. FY27E P/E of 20x Is Cheap If Consensus Is Right**
FY27E EPS consensus: $19.27. At $385.73, that is 20.0x forward — the cheapest the stock has ever been on a forward basis. FY26E EPS consensus of $11.61 implies 33.2x — at peer median. AVGO's gross margin (76.3%) leads the peer group. FCF compounding at 30%+ annually with $11.1B annual dividends and active buybacks.

## Bear Case in One Paragraph

**The core risk is that the stock prices in near-perfect execution across two simultaneously high-stakes transitions — AI ASIC ramp and VMware integration — while offering minimal margin of safety.** The FY27E revenue consensus of $169.72B (a $85B swing between the low and high estimate) requires Broadcom to nearly triple its semiconductor revenue in two years, which would demand ASIC market share that no single custom silicon vendor has historically achieved. If even one hyperscaler (Alphabet is most likely, given partial in-house TPU design) pulls a next-generation ASIC program in-house, Broadcom loses $5–15B in annual design-win revenue with no near-term replacement. Simultaneously, VMware's 2–5x post-acquisition price increases have triggered competitive evaluations by enterprise customers toward Nutanix, Red Hat virtualization, and cloud migration — churn rates are not disclosed, making deferred revenue ($13B) a lagging indicator, not a moat. Layer on $64.9B in gross debt, $7.6B in annual SBC (28% of reported FCF), and a GAAP trailing P/E of 64x, and the margin of safety is thin. The DCF bear scenario ($203/share, –47% from current) requires only a modest deceleration in AI ASIC revenue growth and 5pp margin compression — well within historical semiconductor cycle norms. Macquarie's June 4, 2026 downgrade to Neutral is the canary: the first sell-side defection after a multi-month consensus upgrade wave is often a regime signal.
