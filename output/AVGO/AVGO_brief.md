## What Broadcom Does

Two segments: (1) Semiconductor Solutions — custom AI XPUs for hyperscalers, Ethernet networking, broadband, wireless (Apple Wi-Fi/BT), storage; (2) Infrastructure Software — VMware cloud platform, mainframe software (CA), cybersecurity (Symantec). Fab-lite model; TSMC manufactures. Fiscal year ends October 31.

---

## Bull Case in One Paragraph

Broadcom is generating real, accelerating AI revenue — not projected revenue. Q2 FY2026 implied revenue of ~$22.2B represents +47.9% YoY growth, re-accelerating from FY2025's +23.9%, confirming the AI custom silicon ramp is genuine and independent of the VMware acquisition effect. TTM gross margins of 76.3% and operating margins of 49% reflect both the AI silicon mix and VMware's transition to high-margin subscriptions. FCF is approximately $32B annualised at 42% margins — among the highest in the S&P 500. At 20x FY2027 non-GAAP EPS of $19.27, the stock is not obviously expensive for a company growing revenue 60%+ with this FCF profile and 8.1x interest coverage. Analyst consensus target of $513-525 implies 33-36% upside.

---

## Bear Case in One Paragraph

Broadcom trades at 64x trailing GAAP P/E and 44.7x EV/EBITDA — multiples that require near-flawless execution of an AI silicon ramp concentrated in three hyperscaler customers (Google, Meta, and one undisclosed) who have every incentive to reduce dependency on any single silicon vendor. The FY2027 revenue estimate range ($114.7B low to $200.1B high) reflects genuine binary uncertainty: if any hyperscaler redirects its XPU program to Marvell or in-house, Broadcom's revenue model is structurally impaired. VMware customer relationships were damaged post-acquisition through aggressive pricing restructuring, and retention rates are not disclosed in public data. $51.9B in net debt leaves limited buffer if FCF disappoints. Near-term EPS revision breadth is net-negative (ratio 0.38-0.44 for current/next quarter), suggesting the street is already trimming near-term numbers while hoping the long-term thesis holds.

## Recommendation: OVERWEIGHT

**Rationale:** At 20x FY2027 non-GAAP earnings with 33-36% analyst upside to consensus target, and a $32B FCF engine deleveraging rapidly toward 1x EBITDA, risk/reward favours long exposure over 12-24 months. The primary risk (hyperscaler customer concentration) is real but not imminent — each XPU design cycle is 2-3 years, and current partnerships are active and generating revenue. Near-term estimate pressure (current quarter revision ratio 0.38) warrants a 50% initiation sizing with capacity to add on any 10-15% pullback.

**Key catalyst:** Q3 FY2026 earnings guidance for AI semiconductor segment (expected August 2026).

**Price to prove the bear wrong:** $450+ with sustained AI segment disclosure.
**Price to prove the bear right:** Any announcement of hyperscaler XPU program defection.
