## Bull Thesis — Top 3 Catalysts

- **AI XPU ramp is multi-year and contractually visible.** Google TPU v6/v7 (Trillium/Ironwood), Meta MTIA gen 3, and a third undisclosed hyperscaler are all in multi-year ASIC supply agreements with AVGO. Management's $60-90B FY2027 AI revenue guidance from three named hyperscalers implies AI grows to 35-50% of revenue. The Q3 FY2026 guide of $29.4B (+84% YoY) is the first verifiable test on 2026-09-03.
- **VMware VCF subscription conversion is contractually forced and ~60% complete.** Approximately 22,000 enterprise customers must convert from perpetual licensing to VCF subscription bundles by mid-2026. Average contract value under VCF is 3-5x higher. The remaining ~40% converting through FY2026-FY2027 creates visible, contracted revenue tailwind.
- **Near-trough valuation relative to peers.** AVGO at 19.9x NTM forward P/E is a 29% discount to the 7-peer median (26.5x) despite leading the group in revenue growth (+65.9% NTM). At $340-360 (if reached), NTM P/E would compress to ~21-22x — an exceptionally attractive entry relative to the earnings ramp underway.

## Bear Case

The bear case centers on three compounding risks that are individually manageable but collectively devastating: (1) **Hyperscaler ASIC internalization** — Google, Amazon, Microsoft, and Apple all have active in-house chip design programs; if even one named hyperscaler reduces outsourced XPU volume by 30-50%, the FY2027 $60-90B AI revenue guidance collapses; (2) **China export control escalation** — at an estimated 22% China revenue exposure, any BIS rule expansion covering AVGO's networking or ASIC products beyond current Entity List restrictions removes $8-12B in revenue with no offsetting substitute market; (3) **VMware enterprise churn above 20%** — the forced transition from perpetual licensing to mandatory VCF bundles is driving cloud migration reviews across large enterprises; AVGO does not disclose VMware ARR or customer retention, making this the most opaque risk in the bear case. The bear price target of $195 is broadly consistent with our SOTP ($193.52) and the DCF bear scenario ($190.89), providing a cross-method anchor.

## Key Risks

- **TSMC CoWoS-S capacity** is the near-term operational bottleneck; any yield excursion or Taiwan geopolitical disruption delays Q3 FY2026 revenue recognition and breaks the $29.4B guide.
- **AI hyperscaler capex cycle reversal in 2027-2028** is the most systemic risk; $250B+ in announced AI capex has a 12-24 month lead time, meaning a 2027 slowdown announcement hits AVGO revenue in FY2028.
- **GAAP/non-GAAP amortization gap ($8.2B/year)** creates permanent valuation ambiguity; if institutional standards shift toward GAAP earnings, the 64x trailing GAAP P/E creates $250-300/share downside.
