## §2 — Company Overview & Business Model

Broadcom Inc. is a global designer and supplier of semiconductor and infrastructure software solutions operating across two primary segments: Semiconductor Solutions (~55% of revenue) comprising networking ASICs, custom AI accelerators (XPUs), storage controllers, wireless/RF and broadband chips; and Infrastructure Software (~45%) anchored by VMware, CA Technologies, Brocade, and Symantec enterprise products.

Business Model Flywheel:
  AI Hyperscaler XPU design wins (3 confirmed programmes)
    -> High-ASP custom silicon (multi-year, sole-source per design cycle)
    -> FCF generation ($27B/yr, 36% margin) -> debt repayment + R&D reinvestment
    -> VMware software annuity (>$4B ARR target) -> recurring cash + cross-sell
    -> Scale in EDA, SerDes IP, packaging -> moat in next XPU re-tender (2-3yr)
    -> [cycle repeats at higher revenue base]

Operating model: fab-lite (TSMC primary foundry). Capex intensity 1.0% of revenue. Software at 70%+ gross margin. Combined model targets FCF/revenue >40%.

### ASIC Moat Migration: Is Google Taking It In-House?

This is the central strategic question. The analysis below integrates public disclosures, product announcements, and analyst channel checks.

What Broadcom provides to Google: AVGO co-designs the XPU interconnect fabric, switch ASICs (Tomahawk/Jericho series), and HBM bridging logic for Google's TPU infrastructure. AVGO does NOT design the TPU compute die -- Google's hardware team has owned that internally since TPU v3 (2018).

Bear signals (moat eroding):
1. Google's Trillium (TPU v5/v6) compute dies are 100% internal -- no AVGO involvement at the compute layer.
2. Google joined the Ultra Ethernet Consortium (2024), signalling intent to reduce dependence on Broadcom's proprietary networking stack.
3. Marvell is qualifying as an alternate network ASIC supplier for Google clusters per analyst channel checks (2025-2026).
4. Google's Axion ARM-based CPU (2024) demonstrates willingness to own the full silicon stack.
5. TPU v6 (Trillium) launch materials contain no Broadcom mention at the chip level.

Bull signals (moat intact):
1. System-level network switching (Tomahawk 5, Jericho 3) is deeply embedded -- 2-3 year minimum re-design cycle to displace.
2. AVGO's SerDes IP, HBM interface expertise, and die-to-die packaging represent 10+ years of co-development with TSMC.
3. Meta's MTIA and Microsoft's Maia still use Broadcom networking fabric (no confirmed switch as of 2026-Q2).
4. XPU interconnect TAM grows as cluster sizes scale, even if AVGO loses the compute die -- more TPUs per cluster means more switching silicon.
5. AVGO's advantage is not the chip itself but system-level co-design, firmware, and ecosystem integration.

Verdict -- Migrating, Not Collapsing:
The moat is in transition. Google has already removed AVGO from the compute die opportunity (never AVGO's primary revenue line). The actual revenue at risk is the switching/fabric/interconnect layer, where Marvell is the credible challenger. The 2027-2029 XPU re-tender window is when displacement risk is highest.

Most likely outcome: partial displacement. Google retains AVGO for some fabric/switching; Marvell gains 20-30% share of new cluster designs. Quantified risk: $2-4B/yr haircut to AVGO's Google revenue by FY2028E. Timeline is similar to Apple wireless: 3+ years from tape-out to mass production, so this is not an FY2026 event.

## §12 — Bull Case (10 Catalysts)

Bull thesis: Broadcom is the only scaled ASIC co-design partner for hyperscaler AI infrastructure; VMware annuity conversion will compound FCF at 30%+ for 3 years; at peer-convention P/E applied to FY2028E earnings, fair value is $500+.

1. AI XPU TAM acceleration confirmed: $330B+ combined hyperscaler capex skews to custom silicon. AVGO primary partner for 3 of top 5 XPU programmes through current design cycle.
2. EPS revision momentum: FY2027E revised from $17.53 to $19.35 (+10.4%) in 90 days. Each $1 revision at 20x = +$20 to target.
3. VMware ARR conversion beats: $4B+ ARR with >85% retention. Each 5pp upside = ~$1B incremental FCF.
4. Deleveraging creates buyback capacity: Net Debt/EBITDA 1.1x and falling. Post-delever, $10B buyback = $0.85 EPS accretion annually.
5. Gross margin expansion to 72%+: Software mix increasing as VMware migrates to subscription.
6. Network fabric grows even if compute internalizes: More TPUs per cluster means more Tomahawk/Jericho silicon. Fabric revenue is not displaced by compute die internalization.
7. 4th hyperscaler XPU win: Amazon Trainium 3, xAI Colossus, or Apple data-centre silicon would trigger consensus revision to $25+ NTM EPS.
8. GAAP amortisation cliff: VMware D&A ($8.8B/yr) declines post-FY2027 as intangibles roll off. GAAP P/E halves at same price -- re-rating catalyst for generalist investors.
9. AI capex super-cycle durability: No guidance cuts in 3 consecutive hyperscaler earnings cycles. Each $1B incremental capex at ~8% AVGO share = +$80M revenue.
10. Non-AI semi recovery: Broadband/enterprise in early recovery (book-to-bill >1.0 since Q4 FY2025). Adds $3-5B incremental FY2026 revenue.

## §13 — Bear Case (10 Thesis-Breakers)

Bear thesis: AVGO is a concentrated AI bet at 63x GAAP P/E with no binding revenue guarantees from its top 3 customers, all of whom are mid-way through in-house silicon programmes to reduce third-party ASIC dependence.

1. Google internalizes XPU networking (v7 cycle): Google's Trillium-successor uses internal or Marvell fabric. -$6-9B/yr revenue = $24-36/share at 20x.
2. Meta MTIA 3 goes fully internal: Monarch Butterfly chip displaces AVGO from MTIA interconnect. -$4-6B/yr.
3. Microsoft Maia 2 chooses Marvell: Marvell's Octeon wins Azure Maia 2 interconnect bid. -$3-5B/yr.
4. VMware churn >15%: Migration to Nutanix, public cloud causes ARR miss. FCF $3-5B below plan.
5. AI capex plateau: Any Mag-7 guidance cut >10% on AI infra causes AVGO FY2027E cuts of 25-40%.
6. Interest rate shock: $64.9B gross debt at 5.5% effective. RF rising to 6%+ re-prices kd and WACC.
7. EV/EBITDA de-rating to peer median: 44.3x -> 25.2x (no earnings change) = $214/share (-44%).
8. Q3 FY2026 miss at $29.4B guide: 3-5% shortfall with soft Q4 guide triggers disproportionate sell. Prior -17% on a beat shows fragility.
9. Apple wireless exit ahead of schedule: Proxima 5G modem in iPhone 17 (September 2026). $8-10B/yr = $1.70-2.10 EPS impact.
10. Regulatory/antitrust: FTC scrutiny, EU/UK VMware conditions unresolved. Structural remedies could force software separation.
