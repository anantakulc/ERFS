## What CoreWeave Does

CoreWeave is the largest independent GPU cloud provider in the United States, built exclusively for AI workloads. It operates 43+ data centers, 1+ GW of active power, ~250,000 NVIDIA GPUs, and holds a unique preferential GPU allocation relationship with NVIDIA (which owns ~11% of the company). CoreWeave IPO'd at $100 on March 28, 2025 and has round-tripped from an all-time high of $187 (June 2025) back to near the offer price.

---

## Bull Case in One Paragraph

CoreWeave is growing faster than any cloud company in history — $1.92B in revenue (FY2024) to $5.13B (FY2025, +168%) to $12–13B guided for FY2026 (+140%) — backed by $99.4B in contracted backlog from investment-grade customers on take-or-pay terms. Nine of the top ten AI model developers run on CoreWeave infrastructure, including OpenAI ($22.4B in commitments), Meta ($21B through 2032), Jane Street ($6B), and Anthropic. NVIDIA's nearly doubled equity stake and CoreWeave's status as the world's first validated deployer of the Vera Rubin NVL72 system (10x inference performance per watt vs. Blackwell) demonstrate a structural, sustained GPU-access moat that no hyperscaler has replicated. At 7x FY2026E EV/Revenue with 140% growth, the stock is attractively priced if the capex deployment converts to revenue on schedule.

---

## Bear Case in One Paragraph

CoreWeave is a leveraged buyout masquerading as a growth company: $35.15B in debt at effective rates of 9.75%–15%, quarterly interest expense of $536M and rising to $650–730M in Q2 2026, GAAP net loss of $740M in Q1 alone, and a capex program of $30–35B in FY2026 — more than 2.5x the year's revenue — ensures deeply negative free cash flow through at least 2027. GPU depreciation is computed over six years on assets whose rental market rates have already fallen 50–70%, flattering reported gross margins by hundreds of basis points versus economic reality. Microsoft represents 67% of FY2025 revenue and is simultaneously building its own Azure AI infrastructure, creating a single-company existential risk. DDTL debt facilities trigger debt service coverage ratio covenants as early as H2 2026 — in a revenue shortfall scenario, these covenant tests could become the forcing function for distressed refinancing or asset sales. DA Davidson cut its PT from $175 to $100 in May 2026; HSBC holds a Reduce rating.

## Key Catalysts to Watch (Next 12 Months)

1. Q2 2026 earnings (est. August 2026): Did revenue come in above the $2.53B midpoint? Any guidance raise is materially bullish.
2. Microsoft revenue share update: First quarter where Microsoft falls below 50% of revenue is a major narrative shift.
3. DDTL 5.0 covenant test (after September 30, 2026): Pass or miss will be the debt bear's primary trigger.
4. FY2026 capex spend track: Any downward revision to capex guidance reads as either (a) demand weakness or (b) disciplined capital allocation — market will need to judge which.
5. Core Scientific integration: $500M in annualized cost savings by end of 2027 needs to start showing in margins.
6. Vera Rubin commercial contracts: First enterprise or hyperscaler contracts signed using the Vera Rubin NVL72 platform will validate premium pricing.

## Bottom Line

CoreWeave is the most asymmetric bet in public AI infrastructure right now — extraordinary upside if the $99B backlog converts and the debt is manageable, but a real downside scenario in which equity holders are residual claimants behind $35B in senior secured creditors. The current ~$98 price is almost exactly at the probability-weighted fair value implied by the three scenarios ($150 weighted PT less a discount for execution risk and illiquidity premium). Initiating at current levels for aggressive growth portfolios is defensible; for capital-preservation mandates, wait for a pullback to $80–$85 to improve the margin of safety.
