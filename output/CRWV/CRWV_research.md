## Company Snapshot

CoreWeave is a purpose-built GPU cloud infrastructure company that went public on Nasdaq on March 28, 2025 at a $100 reference price. Founded in 2017 as an Ethereum mining operation, it pivoted to GPU cloud services in 2019 and has since grown into the largest independent AI cloud provider in the United States. The company operates 43+ data centers with more than 1 gigawatt of active power and approximately 250,000 NVIDIA GPUs. It is majority-controlled by Magnetar Capital and counts NVIDIA as both a preferred partner and a ~11% equity holder.

## CHARLIE: The Bull Case

### Thesis Statement
CoreWeave is the defining infrastructure platform for the AI training-to-inference supercycle. It holds structural advantages — preferential NVIDIA GPU access, take-or-pay contracts locking in $99B in future revenue, and a customer roster comprising 9 of the top 10 AI model developers — that no legacy hyperscaler has replicated. Trading at ~7x forward revenue on $12B+ 2026 guidance, it is one of the only public ways to own the GPU cloud layer of AI at growth-company economics.

### Supporting Evidence

**1. Revenue velocity is unprecedented in cloud history.**
CoreWeave grew from $1.92B (FY2024) to $5.13B (FY2025) — a 168% increase — making it the fastest cloud platform to reach $5B in annual revenue. Q1 2026 came in at $2.08B (+112% YoY), beating consensus of $1.97B. Management guides $12B–$13B for FY2026, implying Q4 exit run rate of ~$4.5B/quarter ($18B annualized). The growth trajectory is front-loaded by capacity deployments, not demand speculation.

**2. The $99.4B backlog is structurally superior to traditional cloud bookings.**
Revenue backlog (remaining performance obligations) reached $99.4B as of March 31, 2026 — up 49% in a single quarter from $66.8B at year-end 2025. Critically, more than 60% is tied to investment-grade counterparties with take-or-pay structures. The backlog represents roughly 8x the FY2026 midpoint guidance, providing visibility that is rare in infrastructure at this growth stage.

Key customer commitments (all primary sources — CoreWeave 8-K filings):
- Microsoft: anchor customer, 67% of FY2025 revenue, declining toward 50%
- OpenAI: up to $22.4B in aggregate commitments (March 2025 $11.9B + May 2025 $4B + September 2025 $6.5B), extending through May 2031
- Meta: $21B multi-year agreement through December 2032 (signed March 2026)
- Jane Street: $6B commitment signed April 15, 2026; plus $1B equity investment at $109/share
- Anthropic: multi-year agreement announced April 2026 (financial terms undisclosed)

**3. NVIDIA's strategic embrace is a genuine moat.**
NVIDIA holds approximately 11% of CoreWeave via a $2B Class A investment in Q1 2026, having nearly doubled its stake from Q4 2025. NVIDIA and CoreWeave have a formal collaboration agreement to accelerate AI factory buildout. CoreWeave received the world's first delivery and full rack-scale validation of the NVIDIA Vera Rubin NVL72 system on June 1, 2026 — ahead of every hyperscaler and competing neo-cloud. The Vera Rubin NVL72 delivers up to 10x better inference performance per watt than Blackwell and reduces inference costs to one-tenth the price per million tokens. First-to-market on each new NVIDIA generation is not symbolic; it locks in the high-margin early months of each platform cycle when customers pay premiums for access.

**4. Customer diversification is advancing rapidly.**
Microsoft was 77% of 2024 revenue and 67% of FY2025 revenue. Management expects Microsoft to fall below 50% in 2026 as the OpenAI, Meta, Jane Street, and Anthropic contracts ramp. The Jane Street deal is particularly notable — it represents financial sector adoption, opening a new vertical beyond AI labs. 9 of the top 10 AI model developers now use CoreWeave, including a relationship with Anthropic which represents a near-complete sweep of the frontier AI model layer.

**5. Gross margins are holding at ~72% despite rapid scaling.**
FY2025 gross margin was ~72.5%; Q1 2026 adjusted EBITDA margin of 56% compares favorably to the capex-heavy infrastructure deployment phase. Adjusted EBITDA reached $3.09B in FY2025 — a 154% increase. The bull case holds that as deployed capacity begins generating revenue from the $30-35B capex program (H2 2026 and beyond), operating leverage will flow through and EBITDA margins expand toward the 60%+ corridor.

**6. Core Scientific acquisition changes the cost structure permanently.**
The July 2025 acquisition of Core Scientific (all-stock, $9B implied value at announcement) gave CoreWeave ~1.3 GW of owned data center power plus 1+ GW of expansion capacity. Management estimates $10B in cumulative future lease savings over 12 years and ~$500M in annualized run-rate cost savings by end of 2027. Owning the physical plant transforms a variable lease-driven cost structure into a fixed, depreciating asset — favorable for long-run margins.

**7. Vera Rubin access widens the moat into inference.**
The inference transition — from compute-intensive training to always-on production inference — is the next leg of AI infrastructure growth. CoreWeave has proactively positioned for this via its MLPerf v6.0 DeepSeek-R1 benchmark submissions and the Vera Rubin NVL72 platform, which is purpose-designed for inference economics. The "training-to-inference gap" product launch (CoreWeave's unified agentic AI stack) closes the loop between training and inference serving, adding enterprise stickiness.

**8. Valuation is reasonable relative to growth.**
At $98/share (~$58.9B market cap, ~$86.8B EV):
- EV/FY2026E Revenue (midpoint $12.5B): ~7.0x
- EV/FY2026E Adjusted EBITDA (est. ~$7B at 56% margin): ~12.4x
- Wall Street consensus: 34 analysts, Moderate Buy, average 12-month PT of $130–$138, high of $167

These multiples sit at a meaningful discount to hyperscalers (AWS trades at ~6x revenue but on $110B+ base) given the 140%+ growth profile.

## KILO: The Bear Case

*Kilo ran independently and saw none of Charlie's output. The following case stands on its own.*

### Thesis Statement
CoreWeave is a debt-fueled GPU rental operation disguised as a platform company. Its "backlog" is contingent, its unit economics are obscured by aggressive accounting, its $35B debt pile is growing faster than cash generation can ever service it, and its single largest customer (Microsoft, 67% of FY2025 revenue) has its own GPU buildout ambitions. At ~$98, equity holders own a leveraged residual claim on depreciating silicon, not a durable software platform. The Kerrisdale Capital short thesis (published September 2025) remains directionally correct even if timing was off.

### Bear Arguments and Thesis-Breakers

**1. The debt burden is existential at current interest rates.**
CoreWeave carries approximately $35.15B in total debt as of Q1 2026 (source: Yahoo Finance key statistics). Q1 2026 interest expense alone was $536M — $2.14B annualized — against a GAAP net loss of $740M in the quarter. Q2 2026 interest expense guidance is $650M–$730M, meaning annualized interest runs $2.6B–$2.9B by mid-year. The company is paying effective rates of 9.75%–15% on asset-backed facilities. This is not growth-company leverage; it is the capital structure of a leveraged buyout stretched thin. Equity investors receive what remains after lenders are paid — which is nothing on a GAAP basis and shrinking on a cash basis.

**2. Capex guidance of $30–35B in FY2026 is structurally irreconcilable with free cash flow.**
CoreWeave spent $14.9B in capex in FY2025 and guided $30–35B for FY2026 against FY2026 revenue of $12–13B. This is a capex-to-revenue ratio greater than 2.5x — the company destroys more than $2 in capital for every $1 in revenue it generates in the year of deployment. Free cash flow is deeply negative and will remain so through at least 2027–2028. When the Q1 2026 earnings release disclosed the $30–35B capex figure (which came in significantly above the $26.9B consensus), the stock fell 10% intraday — the market is imperfectly pricing this risk.

**3. GPU depreciation accounting is aggressive and flatters margins.**
CoreWeave depreciates GPUs over a six-year useful life using straight-line methodology. H100 GPU spot rental rates have already declined 50–70% from peak market rates. If useful life is 4 years (consistent with NVIDIA's generational cadence — Hopper to Blackwell to Vera Rubin in under 2 years), reported gross margins overstate economic reality by several hundred basis points. The Kerrisdale report (September 2025) calculated that illustrative EBIT margins of ~20% under CoreWeave's assumptions collapse to ~0% under more conservative depreciation. Management's "illustrative unit economics" deck, per Kerrisdale, was built on "utopian" utilization assumptions per a former engineer. These numbers have not been independently audited at the unit economics level.

**4. Customer concentration is a single-company risk, not a diversification story.**
Microsoft at 67% of FY2025 revenue is the dominant bear-case trigger. Microsoft is simultaneously CoreWeave's largest customer and its most formidable potential competitor: Azure AI infrastructure is scaling rapidly, and Microsoft has the financial resources to reduce external GPU procurement as internal capacity comes online. A 20-percentage-point decline in Microsoft's revenue share — from 67% to 47% — would imply a $1B+ revenue hole unless offset by equal or greater ramp from OpenAI, Meta, Jane Street, and others. The take-or-pay protections are real, but "take-or-pay" contracts can be renegotiated in extremis, especially by investment-grade counterparties with leverage. The OpenAI relationship is also a risk: OpenAI is CoreWeave's second-largest customer and simultaneously receives investment and GPU supply from Microsoft — creating a triangular dependency that could redirect spend.

**5. The backlog is a forward commitment, not cash in hand.**
The $99.4B backlog figure is frequently cited as the bull's anchor. However: (a) take-or-pay structures have exclusions for force majeure, data center failure, and capacity unavailability; (b) the backlog is distributed over multi-year periods, not concentrated in the next 12 months; (c) actual ramp from new contracts (Meta, Jane Street, Anthropic) depends on CoreWeave deploying $30–35B in capex successfully and on schedule — any execution slip compresses recognized revenue; (d) the backlog contains no cash — until infrastructure is deployed and capacity is consumed, revenue cannot be recognized.

**6. ROIC is structurally negative — growth destroys equity value.**
Kerrisdale's central contention: CoreWeave's return on invested capital (ROIC) is below its cost of capital, meaning every dollar of incremental capex deployed destroys equity value. The high-yield debt carries 9.75%–15% coupons. If ROIC on deployed GPU infrastructure is 8–10% (gross return before depreciation, interest, and operating costs), the company is in a value-destruction loop funded by equity dilution and incremental leverage. This is not a phase-of-growth problem; it is a structural problem if GPU rental rates continue to compress as supply scales.

**7. Competitive moat is narrower than NVIDIA preference implies.**
CoreWeave's advantage rests on NVIDIA GPU access and operational GPU expertise. Both are eroding: (a) Lambda Labs, Crusoe Energy, Nebius, and CoreWeave competitors all have access to NVIDIA hardware, just with later allocation; (b) AMD MI300X and MI350X GPUs are entering the market with competitive price-performance profiles; (c) Google TPU v5 and AWS Trainium chips offer hyperscaler-specific alternatives at scale; (d) CoreWeave cannot prevent NVIDIA from expanding allocations to competing GPU clouds if competitive dynamics favor it. Being "first" on Vera Rubin is a 6-to-9-month advantage, not a permanent one.

**8. The DDTL debt covenant structure creates a trip-wire.**
The DDTL 4.0 ($8.5B, March 2026) requires a debt service coverage ratio of at least 1.15x beginning after June 30, 2027. The DDTL 5.0 ($3.1B, May 2026) has a debt service coverage ratio covenant starting after September 30, 2026. With $29.8B in pre-Q1 disclosed debt and Q2 2026 guidance of $650–730M in quarterly interest expense, the math on DSCR covenants is thin. Any revenue shortfall relative to deployed-capacity expectations in H2 2026 / H1 2027 creates covenant risk. A covenant breach on facilities collateralized by GPU assets whose spot rental market is declining would be the functional equivalent of a leveraged real estate margin call.

**9. Q2 2026 guidance miss signals execution risk is arriving on schedule.**
CoreWeave's Q2 2026 revenue guidance midpoint of $2.53B missed the Street's $2.69B consensus. This is consistent with a pattern where capacity deployments are delayed or revenue recognition lags the capex deployment curve. If Q3 and Q4 2026 also guide below consensus, the $12–13B annual guidance becomes at risk — and the stock is priced to perfection on execution.

**10. DA Davidson and HSBC are dissenting, and the reasons are valid.**
DA Davidson cut from Buy to Neutral on May 18, 2026, slashing its price target by $75 (from $175 to $100). HSBC maintains a Reduce rating. These are not reflexive pessimists — they are raising structural concerns about the ratio of capital destroyed to value created. At $98, the stock is at DA Davidson's new price target, implying the most constructive of the skeptics sees no upside from here.

## SYNTHESIS: Recommendation and Probability-Weighted Scenarios

### Our View

CRWV is trading at an inflection point. The IPO reference price of $100 is now a resistance/support level; the stock has round-tripped from an all-time high of $187 (June 2025) back to the offer price, reflecting the market's genuine uncertainty about whether the bull or bear thesis resolves first.

The bull case is real: the backlog is the largest in GPU cloud history, NVIDIA's preferential relationship is documented, and the customer roster (OpenAI, Meta, Jane Street, Anthropic) is expanding beyond Microsoft dependency. The bear case is also real: the debt structure is extreme by any standard, the capex-to-revenue multiple is unsustainable near-term, and the equity value is a thin call option on the gap between $35B+ in debt claims and the NPV of future cash flows.

**Recommendation: HOLD / SELECTIVELY BUY on dips below $85.**
The stock at $98 is priced at roughly fair value given current information. The upside is skewed to the bull case (150%+ from here to $245 in the base bull scenario) but the downside to the bear scenario is severe (50–70% loss). For investors with a 3–5 year horizon and high risk tolerance, initiating a position on weakness toward $80–$85 improves the risk/reward materially. For risk-averse investors, the combination of deeply negative free cash flow, $35B debt, and covenant risk makes the position unsuitable.

**12-month price target (base case): $140**
Methodology: Apply 7.5x EV/Revenue to FY2026E midpoint of $12.5B ($93.75B EV), subtract net debt of $32.88B, divide by 545.6M shares = ~$112 on 2026 numbers. On a 2027E forward basis (est. $20B revenue at growth deceleration to 60%), apply 6x EV/Revenue ($120B EV minus $32.88B net debt / 545.6M shares) = ~$160. Blend the two with equal weight: ~$136, round to $140. Conservative relative to Street consensus of $130–$138.

### Three Probability-Weighted Scenarios

#### Scenario 1 — Bull: AI Capex Supercycle Sustains, Execution Holds
**Probability: 40%**

**Conditions required:**
- FY2026 revenue comes in at $12.5B+ (within guidance range)
- Capex of $30–35B deploys on schedule and capacity is monetized in H2 2026 / 2027 as backlog converts
- Microsoft revenue share declines to ~45–50% without absolute revenue loss (offset by OpenAI, Meta, Jane Street ramp)
- GPU rental rates stabilize or recover modestly as inference demand overwhelms near-term supply additions
- Debt covenants are not triggered; management refinances high-yield debt at lower rates as revenue scales and asset quality improves
- NVIDIA Vera Rubin NVL72 first-to-market status drives premium pricing in H2 2026 contracts

**12-month price target in this scenario: ~$200–$240**

Rationale: In a smooth execution environment, the $18–19B exit ARR guidance for end-2026 places the company on a trajectory toward $20–25B in FY2027 revenue. At 8x EV/2027E Revenue of $22B = $176B EV, minus $33B net debt, divided by 545.6M shares = ~$264. Discount 10% for execution risk: ~$240. Stock re-rating to platform/hyperscaler comps (which trade at 6–10x revenue) is the driver.

#### Scenario 2 — Base: Grinding Execution, Debt Overhang Persists
**Probability: 40%**

**Conditions required:**
- FY2026 revenue comes in near the lower end of guidance ($12.0–12.5B); Q2 guidance midpoint miss signals a pattern
- Capex deployment encounters modest delays; H2 2026 revenue acceleration is below the $18–19B exit ARR management target
- Microsoft concentration continues declining but OpenAI / Meta ramp is back-loaded to 2027
- Gross margins compress 200–300 bps as older GPU inventory ages and newer platform transitions create pricing resets
- Debt covenant tests in H2 2026 pass but are uncomfortably close; refinancing occurs at rates modestly below current
- Free cash flow remains deeply negative through end of 2027

**12-month price target in this scenario: $130–$145**

Rationale: This is consistent with current Street consensus ($130–$138). Market values the asset at 6–7x forward revenue while applying a persistent discount for the debt overhang and FCF negativity. The stock re-rates modestly upward as backlog converts to revenue, but remains below its June 2025 ATH of $187.

#### Scenario 3 — Bear: Debt Covenant Stress, Customer Attrition, or Macro Reversal
**Probability: 20%**

**Conditions required:**
- Microsoft announces a reduction or restructuring of its CoreWeave GPU contracts, or Microsoft's internal Azure AI buildout substitutes for CoreWeave capacity ahead of schedule
- GPU rental market continues to deflate (H100 spot rates down 50%+ from peak); CoreWeave faces pricing pressure on renewals
- One or more DDTL covenant tests is missed or is waived at punitive terms, triggering credit market concern and restricting further draws
- Macro AI investment cycle pulls back: if hyperscaler AI capex guidance is revised downward (due to AI ROI concerns, tariff impacts on GPU supply chains, or regulatory AI restrictions), CoreWeave's backlog customers exercise force majeure or renegotiation clauses
- FY2026 revenue guidance is cut to $10–11B; capex program is partially paused

**12-month price target in this scenario: $40–$55**

Rationale: In a credit-stress / demand-reversal scenario, the equity is a junior residual claim on ~$86.8B EV against $35.15B in debt. A 30% discount to current EV produces an EV of ~$60.8B, minus $35B debt = $25.8B equity, divided by 545.6M shares = ~$47. The stock spent time in the $63–$80 range during the post-Kerrisdale selloff in late 2025; a covenant event or Microsoft attrition would reset the floor much lower. Kerrisdale's $6–$13 "fair value" is plausible only in an extreme asset liquidation scenario and not our base bear case.
