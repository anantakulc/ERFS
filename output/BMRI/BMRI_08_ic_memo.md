# Bank Mandiri (BMRI.JK) — Investment Committee Memo

## 1. Recommendation & Call
**HOLD, target Rp 4,750 (+17% price, ~25% total return).** BMRI is the cheapest ROE-adjusted name among Indonesia's big-four — ~1.2x P/BV on a ~21% ROE franchise with the cleanest credit book in its history (gross NPL 0.98%) and the fastest 1Q26 earnings growth (+16.6% YoY). On raw Gordon-Growth logic (ROE 20%, ke 13.0%, g 4.4%) the justified P/BV is ~1.81x → Rp ~6,450, far above the market. We do **not** rate it Buy because that gap is a structurally-warranted governance/SOE discount: the Danantara sovereign-fund restructuring has changed the minority-shareholder regime, and management has cut NIM guidance. The probability-weighted blend is Rp ~5,325 (Hotel §7.2); we adopt a conservative Rp 4,750 (≈1.33x FY26E BVPS), below the pre-BI-hike sell-side mean of Rp 5,699. The ~7.7% recurring dividend yield is the core of the expected return.

## 2. Variant Perception
Consensus (17 buy/2 sell, mean Rp 5,699) treats BMRI as a cheap quality long and applies a pre-20-May-2026 cost of equity. Our variant view (Hotel §7.1): the sell-side underweights (a) that the +16% 1Q26 prints are a **BRIS-deconsolidation accounting artefact**, not organic re-acceleration (organic guide is only 7–9%), and (b) the **Danantara capital-extraction risk** — the bank's asset-management subsidiary was already sold to Danantara AM in April 2026, and the fund carries a ≥5%/$50bn annual government-transfer mandate. We price a higher CoE and a persistent governance discount; the market prices a clean re-rating.

## 3. Investment Pillars
- **Pillar 1:** Cleanest asset quality of the big-four — gross NPL 0.98%, net 0.41%, declining cost of credit [Charlie §7].
- **Pillar 2:** Deep ROE-adjusted value — ~1.2x P/BV at ~21% ROE vs a raw GGM justified 1.81x [Hotel §7.3].
- **Pillar 3:** ~7.7% recurring dividend yield provides high carry while awaiting re-rating [Charlie §12].
- **Pillar 4:** Livin'/Kopra digital ecosystem — the most durable, internally-controlled moat among state banks [Charlie §13 / Golf §6.2].

## 5. Bear Case Summary (Mandatory)
Delta's case (§3.2): the headline growth is a BRIS-deconsolidation illusion — organic loan growth is only 7–9% and management *cut* FY26 NIM guidance to 4.5–4.7%, the 3rd of 6 recent commitment misses. The Danantara regime is a structural minority-shareholder negative: state ownership moved to PTBKI and franchise assets (the AM subsidiary) have already been sold out of the listed entity, with a government cash-extraction mandate. Post-BI-hike CoE (~13.3%) plus a wholesale-tilted, rate-sensitive funding base (CASA only ~68%) means the NIM-recovery and re-rating thesis fails; at ROE 18–19% and a governance haircut, justified P/BV falls to ~1.0–1.1x → **Rp ~3,300 (−19%)**, cushioned only partly by the dividend.
