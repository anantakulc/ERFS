# BMRI — Stage 2: 19-Point Fundamentals Checklist (bank-charlie)
**Input:** Bravo thesis. All figures `[NON-CAPIQ]`. Currency: Rp. Date: 10 Jun 2026.

## §1 Franchise & Market Position
Largest Indonesian bank by assets (~Rp 2,470tn FY25). State-owned (Persero); flagship of the SOE banking cluster now under Danantara/PTBKI. Universal bank: wholesale (corporate/commercial) core + growing retail (payroll, mortgage, auto via Mandiri Tunas Finance, Livin' super-app ~25mn+ users, Kopra wholesale platform). Strong transaction-banking and cash-management moat with SOE/large-corporate ecosystem.

## §2 Loan Book Composition (FY25 gross ~Rp 1,895tn)
| Segment | % of Total | YoY | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Corporate | ~38% | +13% | Largest; SOE + private large-cap |
| Commercial | ~18% | +14% | Mid-corporate |
| SME | ~10% | +11% | Targeted growth |
| Consumer (mortgage, auto, payroll, cards) | ~22% | +12% | Livin'-driven |
| Micro (incl. KUR) | ~12% | +10% | Subsidised; policy-linked |
**Concentration flag:** Wholesale/SOE-contractor weighting far above BBCA. The system's Rp ~121tn impaired state-contractor segment is a BMRI-relevant tail risk (Delta input).

## §3 Deposits & Funding (FY25)
Deposits Rp 2,106tn (+23.9%); CASA Rp 1,431tn (+12.6%); CASA ratio ~68%. LDR ~90% (higher than BBCA's ~76% — less liquidity cushion). Cost of funds rising with BI at 5.25%; wholesale TD reliance makes BMRI more rate-sensitive than BBCA.

## §4 Fee & Non-Interest Income
Non-NII driven by transaction banking, FX/treasury, wealth, and digital (Livin'/Kopra) fees. Recurring fee momentum solid; treasury gains lumpy. Fee income ~20% of total revenue. Loss of BRIS sharia fee streams post-deconsolidation is a modest drag.

## §5 Recent Results
**FY25:** NP Rp 56.3tn (+0.9% YoY — flat, high base); NII Rp 106.2tn (+4.4%); interest income +8.7%, interest expense +17.6% (cost-of-funds squeeze); loans +13.4%; deposits +23.9%; ROE ~21%; dividend yield ~7.7%.
**1Q26 (post-BRIS deconsolidation 1 Jan 2026):** NP Rp 15.4tn (+16.6% YoY) — beat on strong NII/PPOP + lower cost of credit; NIM 4.7% (guidance cut to 4.5–4.7% FY26 from BRIS exit + global pressure); gross NPL 0.98%, net NPL 0.41% (cycle-low); loan growth +16.2% YoY (BRIS base-effect distorted; organic FY26 target 7–9%).

## §6 NIM & Rate Sensitivity
Reported NIM ~5.0% FY25 → 4.7% 1Q26; FY26 guidance 4.5–4.7%. Two compressors: (1) BRIS deconsolidation removes high-yield sharia margin; (2) BI 5.25% + wholesale funding lifts cost of funds. Asset-yield repricing on higher IDR govvies provides partial offset. Lower CASA ratio than BBCA = thinner NIM buffer.

## §7 Asset Quality
**Strongest in BMRI history:** gross NPL 0.98% (1Q26), net NPL 0.41%, well-provisioned. Restructured/LAR book has normalised post-COVID. Tail risk remains SOE-contractor and commodity-cyclical corporate names. Cost of credit declining — a quality earnings driver.

## §8 Capital
Total CAR ~20%+, CET1/Tier-1 comfortably above OJK minimum. Surplus capital exists but is a double-edged sword under Danantara (extraction risk vs. organic deployment).

## §9 Profitability / ROE
ROE ~21% (ttm) — top-tier, near BBCA, well above BBNI. ROA ~2.5–2.7%. Driven by scale, wholesale efficiency, low credit cost. Sustainability hinges on NIM defense and avoiding ROE-dilutive directed lending.

## §10 Efficiency
Cost-to-income ratio ~38–40% — competitive; digital (Livin'/Kopra) driving operating leverage. Improving trend.

## §11 Growth Outlook
Organic loan growth guided 7–9% FY26 (down from 13.4% FY25). Digital ecosystem + wholesale pipeline (FDI, infrastructure, downstreaming/nickel) supports mid-cycle. BRIS exit lowers consolidated growth base.

## §12 Dividend / Capital Return
Payout ~78% (range cited 78–90%); trailing yield ~7.7% — among the highest of the big-four; a core total-return pillar. **Danantara risk:** SOE dividend policy now intermediated by the sovereign fund's extraction mandate (≥5%/$50bn govt transfer target) — payout could be pushed higher (yield-positive) or capital redirected (governance-negative).

## §13 Digital / Ecosystem
Livin' (retail super-app, 25mn+ users) + Kopra (wholesale) are genuine differentiators driving CASA, fees, and cost efficiency. Best-in-class state-bank digital franchise.

## §14 Management & Governance
Persero SOE; board includes government-appointed commissioners; CEO Darmawan Junaidi-era continuity (verify current). Governance discount vs. private BBCA is structural. Danantara restructuring (Feb 2025–2026) is the dominant governance variable.

## §15 Regulatory
OJK-regulated; BI monetary policy (5.25%). Exposed to directed-lending mandates (KUR micro, cooperative lending, subsidised programs) under Prabowo populist agenda — ROE headwind risk higher than for private peers.

## §16 ESG / Structural
SOE status = state-policy-tool risk; downstreaming/coal/nickel corporate exposure raises ESG-screen friction for some global allocators.

## §17 Liquidity / Float
~40% free float; large, liquid IDX constituent; significant MSCI Indonesia weight → exposed to EM/Frontier reclassification flow risk (shared sector overhang).

## §18 Valuation Anchors (handoff to Hotel)
P/BV ~1.2x; ROE ~21%; FY26E BVPS ~Rp 3,560; DPS ~Rp 495. GGM with ROE 20%, CoE 12.5%, g ~4.4% → justified P/BV ~1.9x — implying material theoretical upside, gated by governance adjustment.

## §19 Bull Pillars (handoff to India)
1. Cleanest credit book of the big-four (gross NPL 0.98%) + fastest EPS growth (+16.6% 1Q26) at the cheapest ROE-adjusted P/BV (1.2x at 21% ROE).
2. ~7.7% dividend yield provides high carry while awaiting re-rating.
3. Livin'/Kopra digital ecosystem driving structural CASA, fee, and efficiency gains.
4. GGM justified P/BV (~1.9x) sits far above market 1.2x — large theoretical re-rating optionality if governance discount narrows.
