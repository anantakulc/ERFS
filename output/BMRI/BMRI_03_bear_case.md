# BMRI — Stage 3: Bear Case / Devil's Advocate (bank-delta)
**Input:** Charlie fundamentals + independent contrarian sourcing. All figures `[NON-CAPIQ]`. Currency: Rp. Date: 10 Jun 2026.

## §3.1 Contrarian Frame
BMRI is the consensus "cheap quality" big-four long. Delta's mandate is to attack that comfort. The bear case is not that the bank is bad — it is that (a) the headline numbers are flattered by accounting, (b) the governance regime has structurally changed against minority holders, and (c) the macro/CoE backdrop caps any re-rating the bulls are underwriting.

## §3.2 Bear Thesis (de-rate path to Rp ~3,200–3,400)

**Bear 1 — The 1Q26 "growth beat" is an accounting illusion (BRIS deconsolidation).**
Headline 1Q26 loan growth of +16.2% YoY and NP +16.6% are materially distorted by the deconsolidation of Bank Syariah Indonesia (BRIS) effective 1 Jan 2026. Organic loan growth guidance is only 7–9% — roughly half the FY25 13.4%. The deconsolidation removes a high-margin sharia book, which is precisely why management *cut* FY26 NIM guidance to 4.5–4.7%. Investors extrapolating the +16% prints into a growth re-acceleration are mispricing a one-off base effect. Strip BRIS and BMRI is a high-single-digit grower with compressing margins.

**Bear 2 — Danantara is a minority-shareholder governance regime change, not a neutral reshuffle.**
State ownership has been transferred to PTBKI (Danantara's operational holding), and on 1 Apr 2026 Danantara Asset Management agreed to **buy the bank's investment-management subsidiary** — value leaving the listed entity. President Prabowo has instructed Danantara to transfer ≥5% return (~$50bn) annually to the government and to redeploy SOE dividends into "high-growth sectors" (renewables, tech, infrastructure). For BMRI minorities this is a structural risk that capital and franchise assets get directed by political priorities rather than ROE. This is the single biggest reason BMRI deserves — and may keep — a governance discount to BBCA.

**Bear 3 — NIM is structurally compressing and BMRI has the thinnest buffer of the quality names.**
FY26 NIM guidance was cut to 4.5–4.7% (from ~5.0% FY25). With CASA ratio only ~68% (vs BBCA ~85%) and a wholesale-tilted, rate-sensitive funding base, BMRI repays the BI 5.25% rate environment faster on the liability side than BBCA. FY25 already showed the squeeze: interest expense +17.6% vs interest income +8.7%, capping NII growth at +4.4%. If BI holds or hikes again to defend the IDR, BMRI's NIM downside is to ~4.4%, costing several trillion Rupiah of NII.

**Bear 4 — SOE-contractor and directed-lending exposure is the sector's structural credit tail, and BMRI sits in it.**
Gross NPL of 0.98% is a cycle low — i.e., it can only deteriorate. BMRI's wholesale book carries the system's elevated SOE-contractor exposure (~Rp 121tn impaired system-wide) and commodity-cyclical corporate names. Under Prabowo, banks face expanding directed/subsidised mandates (KUR micro at capped yields, cooperative lending, ultra-micro). A normalising credit cycle plus mandated below-market lending could lift cost of credit 30–50bps and pull ROE from 21% toward 18%.

**Bear 5 — The re-rating thesis dies on CoE; post-BI-hike, 1.2x P/BV is roughly fair, not cheap.**
The GGM "justified 1.9x" relies on a benign CoE. Post-20 May 2026 BI hike to 5.25%, with the 10yr IDR govvie ~7%, beta ~1.0–1.1 and a justified SOE-governance risk premium, BMRI's CoE is ~13–13.5%. Plug CoE 13.3%, ROE 19% (mid-cycle), g 4.2% into (ROE−g)/(CoE−g) → justified P/BV ~1.6x; apply a governance haircut and you land near the current 1.2–1.3x. The stock is not obviously mispriced — it is fairly valued for a high-yield SOE in a hostile macro.
