# BMRI — Stage 1: Thesis & Quantitative Snapshot (bank-bravo)
**Ticker:** BMRI.JK | **Date:** 10 June 2026 | All figures `[NON-CAPIQ]` per Stage 0. Currency: Indonesian Rupiah (Rp).

## §1 Cycle Context & Core Thesis

Bank Mandiri is Indonesia's largest bank by assets (FY25 total assets ~Rp 2,470tn) and the flagship state-owned (Persero) wholesale-led universal bank. The thesis sits at the intersection of three forces:

1. **Operational momentum is genuinely strong** — 1Q26 net profit grew +16.6% YoY to Rp 15.4tn, a clean beat driven by strong NII/PPOP and lower cost of credit, with gross NPL at a cycle-low 0.98%. This is the best asset-quality print in BMRI's modern history.
2. **The valuation has de-rated to a deep discount** — at Rp 4,060 the stock trades at ~1.2x P/BV for a ~21% ROE franchise, a ~24% drawdown from its 52-week high of Rp 5,375, with a ~7.7%+ trailing dividend yield. On a GGM basis a 21% ROE / ~12.5% CoE franchise screens cheap at 1.2x.
3. **But two BMRI-specific structural overhangs cap the re-rating** — (a) the **BRIS (Bank Syariah Indonesia) deconsolidation** effective 1 Jan 2026 inflates headline 1Q26 loan growth (+16.2%) to an optically misleading level (underlying organic target is only 7–9%), and (b) the **Danantara** sovereign-fund restructuring transfers state ownership to a holding vehicle (PTBKI) and has already extracted the bank asset-management subsidiaries — introducing a dividend-extraction and capital-allocation governance risk unique to SOE banks.

**Core variant view:** The market correctly prices BMRI cheaper than BBCA (1.2x vs 2.1x P/BV) for governance/SOE reasons, but is now over-discounting a bank delivering the cleanest credit book and fastest earnings growth among the big-four. The base case is a HOLD with a positive total-return skew: the ~7.7% dividend yield plus modest re-rating toward ~1.3–1.4x P/BV supports a Rp ~4,700–4,900 target, but the Danantara dividend-policy uncertainty and post-BI-hike CoE prevent a higher-conviction BUY until the FY26 dividend and FY27 organic loan trajectory are confirmed.

## §3 Loan Book (FY25, consolidated pre-BRIS-deconsolidation)
Wholesale-led: corporate + commercial ~55–60% of gross loans; SME ~10%; consumer/micro (incl. KUR, mortgages, payroll, Mandiri Tunas Finance auto) ~30%. Total gross credit Rp 1,895tn FY25 (+13.4%). Post-BRIS deconsolidation (1 Jan 2026) the sharia book exits, lowering the base; 1Q26 +16.2% YoY is a base-effect artefact, not organic acceleration. Concentration risk: SOE-contractor and large-corporate exposure (the sector's structural impaired segment) is materially higher than at private-bank BBCA — a key Delta input.

## §4 Deposit & Funding Snapshot (FY25)
Total deposits Rp 2,106tn (+23.9% YoY — partly BRIS-inclusive). CASA Rp 1,431tn (+12.6%), CASA ratio ~68% (structurally below BBCA's ~85%; above BBNI). Wholesale-tilted funding base means higher rate-sensitivity of cost of funds than BBCA — a NIM-compression vulnerability as BI holds at 5.25%.
