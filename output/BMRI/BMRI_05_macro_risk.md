## §5.1 Macro Backdrop (Indonesia, shared sector cycle)
- **Bank Indonesia policy rate 5.25%** after a surprise +50bps hike on 20 May 2026 to defend the IDR — a regime reversal from the prior easing expectation; raises bank CoE sector-wide.
- **IDR at/near record lows** (~Rp 16,600–17,600/USD range in 2026) — erodes USD-equivalent returns for foreign holders, constrains BI accommodation, pressures imported inflation.
- **Fitch sovereign outlook Negative** (Mar 2026); foreign equity outflows persistent.
- **Prabowo populist fiscal agenda** — subsidised ultra-micro loans, KUR expansion, cooperative/directed lending, 40-year mortgage tenors — quasi-fiscal burden on banks, heaviest on SOE banks like BMRI.

## §5.4 Net Assessment
BMRI's macro/structural risk profile is dominated by two SOE-specific factors — Danantara governance and directed-lending — layered on the shared Indonesian macro stress (IDR, BI hike, MSCI). These justify a persistent governance discount to BBCA but are largely already in the ~1.2x P/BV. The asymmetric tail risks (Danantara extraction, MSCI reclassification) are the reasons to cap conviction at HOLD rather than BUY.
