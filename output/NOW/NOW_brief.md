## Bear Case
ServiceNow's revenue scales with *human seats*, and agentic AI structurally threatens that model — if agents replace users, seat counts and per-seat pricing deflate, and consumption pricing may not fully offset. The warning signs are real: EPS beat magnitude collapsed from +14.6% (Q2'25) to +0.4% (Q1'26) as the bar caught up, and NOW still trades at a clear premium to a de-rating software group (8.2x EV/Rev vs 5.6x peer median; 39.8x EV/EBITDA vs 18.1x). Mean-reversion to peers implies ~$79 (-31%). With 88.6% institutional ownership and 69–81% of DCF value in terminal value, any growth-fund consensus break or rate-regime shift hits this long-duration name hard — as the -46% drawdown from highs already shows.

## Key Risks
- AI agents erode seat-based subscription revenue (Medium probability / High impact).
- Valuation mean-reversion to peer multiples (-31% to ~$79).
- GAAP earnings thin (trailing P/E 68x); SBC dilution offsets buybacks — the cheap 22.7x is non-GAAP.
